**📌 Cybersecurity Resources**

**🆓 Free Resources**

🔗 **roadmap.sh — Cyber Security Roadmap**  
[link](https://roadmap.sh/cyber-security) — A good starting point if you don't know where to begin, or what order to learn things in.

🔗 **Professor Messer — Security+ (YouTube, free)**  
[link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KiEptGbnEBc&list=PLG49S3nxzAnl4QDVqK-hOnoqcSKEIDDuv) — Really good resource in my opinion. I know youtube videos can be a weird spot when trying to learn, but I used his videos to study for the [ISC2 CC(Certified in Cyber Security)](https://www.isc2.org/certifications/cc), which is pretty much a baby version of the CompTIA Security+.  A lot of people I know recommend his videos and tons of people pass using just these videos.

🔗 **Addie LaMarr's Cybersecurity Vault (Obsidian Publish)**  
[link](https://publish.obsidian.md/addielamarr/01+Cybersecurity+Mastery) — This is a public knowledge base that covers cybersecurity concepts, tools, and frameworks. Great to use as a reference when you hit an unfamiliar term. Not really a course.


───────── **💰 PAID RESOURCES 💰** ─────────

🔗 **TryHackMe**  
[link](https://tryhackme.com) — free tier has some beginner friendly hands on labs if you want to actually practice, but if you want to actually get some use out of the app you would need to pay. Their premium tier is $17 monthly, and their Max tier is $31 a month.

🔗 **HTB Academy**  
[link](https://academy.hackthebox.com) — A place with structured cybersecurity courses from Hack The Box. Could be a good entry point if you want something beginner friendly before jumping into the main platform. Here is the link to a page going over the different subscriptions: [link](https://help.hackthebox.com/en/articles/13677074-academy-subscriptions)

🔗 **Hack The Box (main platform)**  
[link](https://www.hackthebox.com) — Give you hands on hacking labs with realistic machines and challenges. Less guided than HTB Academy or TryHackMe, and it expects you to problem solve on your own. Great for building real skills once you have the fundamentals down. HTB is well respected and your ranks/achievements that you get carry weight that you can add on a resume. Here is the link to a page going over the different subscriptions: [link](https://help.hackthebox.com/en/articles/7257535-htb-labs-subscriptions)
