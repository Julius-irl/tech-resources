**📌 Frontend Engineering Resources**

**🆓 Free Resources**

🔗 **roadmap.sh — Frontend Developer Roadmap**  
[link](https://roadmap.sh/frontend) — A good starting point if you don't know where to begin, or what order to learn things in.

🔗 **freeCodeCamp — Responsive Web Design, JavaScript, & Front-End Dev Certs**  
[Responsive Web Design](https://www.freecodecamp.org/learn/responsive-web-design-v9/)
[JavaScript](https://www.freecodecamp.org/learn/javascript-v9/)
[Front-End Development Libraries](https://www.freecodecamp.org/learn/front-end-development-libraries-v9/)
— These are project based, and free courses, with projects that lead to certifications at the end. These are considered the strongest free path into frontend. Good starting point for absolute beginners.

🔗 **The Odin Project**  
[link](https://www.theodinproject.com) — A free and open source full stack curriculum. They have a strong community and real projects as well. A great contender to freeCodeCamp. They have a different teaching style, but you learn the same fundamentals.

🔗 **MDN Web Docs**  
[link](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/) — A documentation by Mozilla for HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

🔗 **JavaScript.info**  
[link](https://javascript.info) — A really good reference and tutorial for JavaScript. This is frequently recommended, and is better than most paid JS books

🔗 **CSS-Tricks + web.dev**  
[CSS-Tricks](https://css-tricks.com) 
[Web.dev](https://web.dev)
— These are articles that go over CSS techniques and performance from professionals.


───────── **💰 PAID RESOURCES 💰** ─────────

🔗 **Frontend Masters**  
[link](https://frontendmasters.com) — They give you courses taught by engineers who have worked at places like Netflix, and Spotify. A good resource if you want to go deeper than where the free resources would typically take you. They do offer a free beginner bootcamp that teaches you HTML, CSS, & JS from scratch.
